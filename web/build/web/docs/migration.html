Bring your data with you

Migrate from Twitter, Instagram, Bluesky, and other platforms. Your data is yours to move.

Why Migrate to Kyron?

You've built an audience and created content on other platforms. Switching shouldn't mean starting from scratch. Kyron makes it easy to bring your data with you—your followers, your posts, your identity.

What You Can Migrate

Depending on the platform, you can migrate:

  • Your profile information (bio, profile picture, links)
  • Your posts and media
  • Your followers and following lists
  • Your likes and bookmarks
  • Your communities and group memberships

Migration from Twitter/X

Step 1: Go to twitter.com/settings/download_your_data and request your data archive. Twitter will email you a link to download your data (usually within 24 hours).

Step 2: In Kyron, go to Settings → Import Data → Twitter. Upload your data archive.

Step 3: Kyron will import your posts, followers, and profile information. This usually takes a few minutes depending on the size of your archive.

Migration from Bluesky

Step 1: Go to bsky.app/settings/export and export your data. Bluesky will download a JSON file with all your data.

Step 2: In Kyron, go to Settings → Import Data → Bluesky. Upload your exported file.

Step 3: Kyron will import your data. Since Bluesky uses AT Protocol (same as Kyron), the import is seamless.

Migration from Instagram

Step 1: Go to instagram.com/download/request and request your data. Instagram will email you a download link (usually within a few days).

Step 2: In Kyron, go to Settings → Import Data → Instagram. Upload your data archive.

Step 3: Kyron will import your posts, followers, and profile. Note: Instagram followers won't automatically follow you on Kyron, but we'll help you invite them.

Inviting Your Followers

After migrating your data, you can invite your followers to join Kyron. We'll generate a custom invite link you can share on your old platform. Your followers can click the link and follow you on Kyron.

AT Protocol Portability

Kyron is built on AT Protocol, the same open standard powering Bluesky. This means your data is truly portable. You can:

  • Use the same identity across multiple AT Protocol apps
  • Switch platforms without losing your identity or data
  • Export your data anytime and use it elsewhere
  • Run your own server if you want complete control

Need Help?

If you run into any issues during migration, our support team is here to help. Email support@kyron.spidroid.com or join our Discord community for assistance.
