AT Protocol Guide

Understand the technology behind Kyron and other decentralized social networks.

What is AT Protocol?

AT Protocol (Authenticated Transfer Protocol) is an open standard for decentralized social networks. It's designed to enable interoperability between different social platforms while maintaining user privacy and data ownership.

Core Concepts

DIDs (Decentralized Identifiers)

A DID is a unique identifier that you control. It's like a username, but you own it cryptographically. Your DID is portable—you can use it on any AT Protocol app.

Personal Data Repositories (PDR)

Your PDR is where your data lives. It's a personal data store that you control. You can run it yourself or use a hosting provider. Your data is encrypted and only you can authorize access to it.

Lexicons

Lexicons define the structure of data in AT Protocol. They're like schemas that describe what a post, profile, or follow looks like. Anyone can define new lexicons.

Federation

AT Protocol is federated, meaning multiple servers can interoperate. You can follow people on different servers, and your data can be replicated across multiple providers.

How It Works

  1. Create Identity: You create a DID and set up your personal data repository
  2. Create Content: You post content to your repository
  3. Share: You share your DID with others
  4. Discover: Apps can discover your content through your DID
  5. Interact: Others can follow you, like, and comment on your posts
  6. Portability: You can move to a different app and keep your identity and data

Key Benefits

  • User Control: You own your identity and data
  • Interoperability: Apps can work together
  • Portability: You can switch apps without losing your data
  • Privacy: Your data is encrypted and only you authorize access
  • Innovation: Anyone can build new apps on AT Protocol

Getting Started

To get started with AT Protocol on Kyron:

  1. Create a Kyron account
  2. Your DID is automatically created
  3. Start posting and connecting with others
  4. Your data is stored in your personal repository
  5. You can export your data anytime
