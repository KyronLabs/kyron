Communities, Not Algorithms

Kyron communities are spaces where people with shared interests gather. Unlike algorithmic feeds, communities are transparent and human-moderated. You know the rules, you know who's in charge, and you have a voice in how the community evolves.

Create Your Own Community

Starting a community is simple. Choose a topic, set your rules, and invite people. You become the moderator and set the culture. Communities can be public or private, open or invite-only.

Human Moderation

Moderation is done by humans, not algorithms. Community leaders decide what's acceptable. They can remove spam, enforce rules, and create a safe space for their members. No hidden algorithms deciding what people see.

Transparent Rules

Every community has public rules. Members know exactly what's expected. If a post is removed, you know why. No surprises, no shadowbanning, no mystery moderation.

Member Roles

Communities can have multiple roles: moderators, contributors, members. Different roles have different permissions. You control who can post, who can moderate, and who can invite new members.

Monetization

Community leaders can monetize their communities. Charge membership fees, sell exclusive content, or receive tips from members. The revenue goes directly to you—no middleman taking a cut.
