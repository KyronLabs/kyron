The Complete Guide to Decentralized Social

Everything you need to know about decentralized social networks, AT Protocol, and why they matter for the future of the internet.

What is Decentralized Social?

Decentralized social networks are platforms where no single company controls the infrastructure. Instead, data is distributed across multiple servers, and users own their own identity and data. This is fundamentally different from traditional social networks like Twitter or Facebook, where the platform owns everything.

The Problems with Centralized Social

Traditional social networks have several critical issues:

  • Data Ownership: You don't own your data. The platform does.
  • Algorithmic Control: Algorithms decide what you see, often prioritizing engagement over truth.
  • Censorship: Platforms can ban you without due process.
  • Monetization: Platforms sell your data to advertisers.
  • Lock-in: You can't take your data elsewhere.

How Decentralized Social Works

Decentralized social networks use open protocols (like AT Protocol) that anyone can build on. Your identity is a cryptographic key that you control. Your data lives on servers you choose or that you run yourself. Applications can read and write to your data, but they can't own it.

AT Protocol: The Standard

AT Protocol is an open standard for decentralized social networks. It's used by Bluesky, Kyron, and other platforms. It provides:

  • User Identity: DIDs (Decentralized Identifiers) that you control
  • Data Repositories: Personal data stores that you own
  • Federation: Multiple servers can interoperate
  • Portability: You can move between platforms

Why Decentralized Social Matters

Decentralized social networks are important for several reasons:

  • Freedom: No platform can censor you arbitrarily
  • Privacy: Your data is yours, not sold to advertisers
  • Choice: You can choose which app to use
  • Innovation: Anyone can build new features
  • Resilience: No single point of failure
